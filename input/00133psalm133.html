A song to sing as we climb.

David wrote this song.

133 It is good for a family of God's people to live together in peace.
Yes! It brings great pleasure!
2 It brings pleasure like valuable olive oil
that is poured on your head,
and it goes down onto your beard.
They poured it onto Aaron's head and his beard,
and it went down onto his clothes. [ a ]
3 It brings pleasure like the dew on Hermon. [ b ]
It makes the mountains of Zion become wet. [ c ]
That is the place where the Lord has promised to bless his people.
He blesses them with life for ever.

Footnotes

  1. 133:2 Aaron was Moses' brother. He was the first priest that God chose to serve him. When the Israelites chose a new priest, they poured olive oil on his head to show that he was a special servant of God.
  2. 133:3 Dew is water that comes onto the ground at night. It helps plants to grow when there is no rain. Hermon is in the north of the land of Israel.
  3. 133:3 Zion was the hill in Jerusalem where they built God's Temple.

A song of David for going up to the Temple.

133 Oh, how wonderful, how pleasing it is
when God’s people all come together as one [ a ] !
2 It is like the sweet-smelling oil that is poured over the high priest’s [ b ] head,
that runs down his beard flowing over his robes.
3 It is like a gentle rain [ c ] from Mount Hermon falling on Mount Zion.
It is there that the Lord has promised his blessing of eternal life.

Footnotes

  1. Psalm 133:1 when … as one Or “when brothers live together in peace.”
  2. Psalm 133:2 high priest’s Literally, “Aaron’s.”
  3. Psalm 133:3 gentle rain Or “mist” or “snow.” The Hebrew can mean either “the oil is like the mist …” or “Aaron’s beard is like the snow ….”
